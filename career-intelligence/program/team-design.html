Career Intelligence — Design at a Glance

Where the program is going, and how each stage feeds the experiment (EDFG) so we collect the data that tests our hypotheses.

The student problem we're designing against: graduating seniors face two acute problems regardless of skill level — a mindset problem (how they think about what they uniquely bring → HVP) and a communication problem (how they connect that value to a specific market → IS). The program moves them from "not sure what I'm aiming at" to a defensible niche, a resume that proves it, and a search they run like an experiment.

The arc

Week 1
Know Your Niche
Know Yourself + Know the Market → a first niche.
Week 2
Sharpen the Niche
10 real jobs → 2–3 role types; Resume v2.
Week 3
Resume Workshop + Dojo
Live critique; verify niche; set up the AI Dojo.
Week 4
Begin Own Your Search
Write the Search Strategy Hypothesis (SSH).
Week 5
Iteration 1 + Fireside
First real search cycle; recruiter fireside.
Week 6
Iteration 2 + Network
Second cycle; networking; synthesis.

Own Your Search is personalized to each student's bottleneck across four levers, so they go deep where it matters rather than shallow everywhere: Positioning — niche + resumeAccess — referrals + outreachProof — a targeted projectPerformance — interviewing

The Dojo is the durable AI-coaching spine — a Core method plus modules (Resume now; Search Strategy, Interview, Networking as we build them). Productive struggle is enforced architecturally: the student makes the decisions; the AI sharpens the thinking and grounds advice in cited, reputable sources.
