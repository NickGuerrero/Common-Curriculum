1 · Welcome & Meet the Team ~2 min

Who’s running Career Intelligence — Nancy, Nicholas, Sathya, and Jeff — and the perspective each brings.

Takeaways

  • The program is run by CTI: Nancy (coordinator), Nicholas (CTI alum), Sathya (CS professor & CTI director), and Jeff (former Microsoft/TikTok recruiter).
  • Jeff brings the industry/recruiter lens; the rest of the team spans teaching and program design.
  • This is the first cohort — explicitly framed as a shared learning experiment for staff and students alike.

2 · How the Program Works ~3 min

The day’s plan, the weekly Monday cadence, why nothing is graded, and using Slack for questions.

Takeaways

  • One live session a week (Mondays); everything you produce gets uploaded to Canvas.
  • Nothing is graded and nothing is busywork — the work is meant to be revisited and built on.
  • Ask questions on Slack rather than email, so the whole community can see the answer.

3 · The Founder’s Mindset ~3 min

Treat your job search like a founder: start with a hypothesis, run small experiments, then adjust.

Takeaways

  • Don’t write one perfect plan and execute it — make a small bet, watch what happens, and adjust.
  • The cycle: hypothesis (“I’m a fit for these roles because…”) → experiment (apply, reach out, talk to people) → adjust.
  • Expect uncertainty — keep moving and treat each application or conversation as a test.

4 · Know Your Niche & Own Your Search ~2.5 min

The two phases of the program — the first three weeks find your niche, the next three run your search.

Takeaways

  • The two phases must happen in order: you can’t own your search before you know your niche.
  • Weeks 1–3 (Know Your Niche): get specific about what work you’re a strong fit for, and why.
  • Weeks 4–6 (Own Your Search): run your search as a weekly cycle — at least three times — and keep going after the program.

5 · Get-to-Know-You Activity ~4 min

The trio breakout — how the reader/answerer/note-taker roles work, and why we keep light notes.

Takeaways

  • A trio breakout with rotating roles — reader, answerer, note-taker — over three short rounds.
  • It’s a conversation, not an interview: pick a few starters, don’t grind through every question.
  • One person submits the shared notes doc — it’s how the team gets to know the community, not a graded artifact.

6 · A Resume Tip: Call Out Your Context ~2 min

Jeff on making your unique context explicit — the “Olympic podium” idea for standing out.

Takeaways

  • Make your unique context explicit — recruiters won’t infer it (e.g., finishing a degree in three years).
  • Frame achievements like an Olympic podium: give the numbers/context (top 3 out of how many?) that move you up the stack.
  • Anything where you went above and beyond is worth naming — it signals you push yourself.

7 · Super Agency + Human Value ~3 min

Partner with AI to do more, but bring your own thinking — create with it, don’t just consume.

Takeaways

  • Use AI to do more, and more impactful, work than you could alone — but partner with it, don’t outsource to it.
  • Your “human value” comes from contributing your own thinking, not submitting what AI produced.
  • A useful test: are you creating with AI, or just consuming what it hands you?

8 · The Symbiotic Thinking Dojo ~6 min

A live demo: how the Dojo asks questions to make you think, instead of handing you a finished answer.

Takeaways

  • The Dojo wraps a normal chatbot in a “guardrail layer” that asks you questions instead of dumping a finished plan.
  • Side by side: ChatGPT makes choices for you; the Dojo makes you think and contribute.
  • It’s an experiment the team wants feedback on, and it runs locally — your chat isn’t stored on a server.

9 · Why Your Niche Matters ~4 min

Sathya answers “why not just apply everywhere?” — aim at a target rather than spraying and praying.

Takeaways

  • In a tough entry-level market, “spray and pray” doesn’t work the way it did pre-COVID — be strategic.
  • Your niche is the overlap of what you’re good at and what the market wants — it sharpens your target, message, and resume.
  • Defining it raises your odds; you can still apply broadly too, but the niche is where you’re most likely to win.

10 · The Human + AI Activity ~3.5 min

The paired Dojo exercise — two humans and one AI, then save your JSON and submit it.

Takeaways

  • A paired exercise: two humans plus one AI — talk through the Dojo’s questions together, not alone with the bot.
  • Take turns sharing your screen; each person runs their own “know yourself” Dojo conversation.
  • Save your conversation as JSON and submit it on Canvas (the “Human + AI Connections” assignment).

11 · This Week’s Work & Wrap-Up ~5.5 min

The week’s activities (handwritten reflection, the Dojos, market research, niche doc, self-check) and the closing.

Takeaways

  • Five activities, ~3 hours total: a handwritten reflection, the Know Yourself Dojo, Know the Market, your Niche Doc, and a self-check.
  • Always share Google Docs as “anyone with link can comment” so the team can give you feedback.
  • It’s a snapshot, not a finish line — you’ll keep updating your niche doc and resume each week; post questions on Slack.
