1 · Recap: The Two Phases ~1.5 min

Where we are: Know Your Niche → Own Your Search.

Takeaways

  • The program has two phases: Know Your Niche (weeks 1–3) and Own Your Search (the next three weeks).
  • Your niche is never “done” — keep refining it as you go.
  • Owning your search means treating applications as something to learn from, not a box to tick.

2 · Run Your Search as an Experiment ~2.5 min

Why an experimental mindset beats “apply and wait.”

Takeaways

  • Most resume and search questions have no fixed answer — test different things and see what works.
  • Embrace uncertainty as a real part of the process, especially with AI and a shifting market.
  • Treat failures and successes as data — after ~20–30 applications with no response, adjust rather than send more of the same.

3 · The Referral Experiment ~6 min

The team’s referral experiment — and how to earn an introduction.

Takeaways

  • A referral gets your resume noticed (not a guaranteed interview) — turning a ~6-second glance into a real look.
  • The bet: make it easy to spot motivated, prepared candidates, and the team’s industry connections keep helping.
  • The process: you build a case for a specific posting → a ~15-min interview with the team → if it’s strong, a warm introduction. The case is yours to make and defend.

The AI-built job board and how to use it.

5 · The Mindset Shift ~2 min

From checklist to learning loop.

Takeaways

  • Stop treating job search as “apply and wait” — it’s a continuous learning process with no fixed formula.
  • To own your search: define a bet (a well-designed test), run it, and adjust from what you see.
  • Being intentional gets you where you want to go faster.

6 · Designing a Bet: Geography & Sector ~2.5 min

Turning applications into a bet you can learn from.

Takeaways

  • A “bet” groups your applications by one property so you can compare — e.g., 10 local vs. 10 elsewhere, or by sector.
  • Don’t just apply to the next 20 random places — split them so you learn something.
  • Pick a property that’s personally meaningful to you (location, sector, company type).

7 · Running the Bet: Comparison Groups ~3 min

Same resume, two groups, read the results.

Takeaways

  • Send the same resume to both groups (e.g., 15 + 15) so the comparison is fair.
  • Watch where responses actually come back — even a job you don’t want teaches you something.
  • “Spray and pray” only works when jobs outnumber candidates; in a tight market, be intentional.

8 · Learning From the Signals ~3 min

Reading the signals and filling the gaps.

Takeaways

  • Look for a skill or phrase that keeps appearing in postings but is missing from your resume (e.g., Docker, “good communicator”).
  • Don’t just edit the resume — take a course or build a project to genuinely add the missing thing.
  • Not hearing back is a lesson — always be learning and building, not just waiting.

10 · Recap + Jeff on Referrals ~3.5 min

The feedback loop, and what a referral really asks of you.

Takeaways

  • The loop: search intentionally → use the signals to update your resume, learning, and projects → repeat until you land a job you’re happy with.
  • A network “bypass” (warm intro) is coming next week to skip the thousand-application pile.
  • Jeff’s caution: a referral flags you for a reason — come “sharper, harder, more elevated,” not with a normal resume.
