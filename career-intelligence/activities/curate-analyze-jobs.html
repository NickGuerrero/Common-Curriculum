Career Intelligence · Week 2 · Develop & Articulate Your Niche

Curate & Analyze 10 Jobs Aligned to Your Niche

Learning goal

Build a base of what employers actually look for, and get your resume and your niche into alignment with it.

Find 10 real job postings where your niche shows up explicitly — not generic software jobs. The words employers repeat are the evidence your niche is real, and the language your resume should speak.

What to do

  1. Find 10 real postings across several organizations where your niche is explicit.
  2. For each, note the niche language and the key requirements they repeat.
  3. Use AI to pull the patterns across all 10 — then check them yourself.
  4. Mark where your resume already matches and where it does not.
Two checks as you go
  • Niche explicit? If you cannot point to the words in a posting that match your niche, it is a generic job — swap it out.
  • Cannot find 10? That is a signal your niche may be too narrow or off. Adjust it rather than force-fit.

See it in action

Worked example — a sports-technology software engineer

Niche: sports-technology software engineer — performance analytics for athletes and teams.

Targets: Whoop, Strava, Garmin, Nike, professional sports organizations.

Niche language to look for in their postings: performance analytics, wearable / sensor data, athlete monitoring, recovery and training-load metrics, time-series data.

Proof it connects: a project like an iOS app that shows an athlete their recovery score, injury risk, and training-performance decline speaks the exact language those postings use — that alignment is the whole point.

Capture & submit

Make a copy of the job tracker, then log your 10 niche-aligned jobs — company, position, status, link, and notes on the requirements they repeat.

Submit: the link to your copy of the tracker (set sharing so anyone with the link can view).
