Career Intelligence · Week 2 · Develop & Articulate Your Niche

Curate & Analyze 10 Jobs Aligned to Your Niche

Learning goal

Find real jobs that fit your niche so your search targets roles you can actually land.

What to do

  1. Start from the job-search prompt below — paste it into your AI tool with your own niche filled in.
  2. Find 10 real postings across several organizations where your niche is explicit, and log them in the tracker.
  3. Pull the requirements they repeat — AI can help, then verify — and mark where your resume matches or doesn’t.
  4. Sort your 10 into 2–3 role types — group the postings that ask for the same kind of work, and name each group in plain words.
Job-search prompt — fill in your niche

I’m an early-career candidate targeting roles as a [YOUR NICHE — e.g., “backend engineer for data-heavy products: APIs, databases, performance”]. Find 10 real, currently-open job postings from different companies where this niche is explicit in the responsibilities or requirements. For each, give the company, title, link, and the 3–5 exact phrases in the posting that match my niche. Then group the 10 into 2–3 role types and tell me, in one line each, what kind of work distinguishes each type. Don’t invent postings — only ones you can link to.

Three checks
  • Niche explicit? If you can’t point to the words that match your niche, it’s a generic job — swap it.
  • Can’t find 10? Your niche may be too narrow — adjust rather than force-fit.
  • Real role types? If a group is just a list of companies, say what the work actually is — or merge it into another type.

See it in action

Worked example — a sports-technology software engineer

Niche: sports-tech software engineer — performance analytics for athletes and teams.

Targets: Whoop, Strava, Garmin, Nike, pro sports organizations.

Niche language to look for: performance analytics, wearable / sensor data, athlete monitoring, recovery and training-load metrics.

Capture & submit

Make a copy of the job tracker, then log your 10 niche-aligned jobs — company, position, status, link, the requirements they repeat, and which of your 2–3 role types each belongs to.

Submit: the link to your copy of the tracker (set sharing so anyone with the link can view).
