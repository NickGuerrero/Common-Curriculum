Set Up Your Resume Dojo

Learning goal

Stand up an AI project that reviews your resume the way a coach would — so you can keep sharpening it on your own, for every role you target.

You could paste a long prompt into a fresh chat every time. But a project lets you set it up once: your instructions and the Dojo files stay loaded, your resume and the job posting stay in context, and you reuse it for every application. Learning to use projects is a skill worth having on its own.

What you’ll need

  • The Dojo files — the Dojo Core and the Resume module, from the Dojo library.
  • Your current resume.
  • One real job posting you’re targeting.

Go deeper (optional): Apply Within — Laszlo Bock’s free job-search playbook (former Google SVP of People Operations); the Dojo’s resume guidance draws on it.

Set it up — pick your tool

The idea is the same everywhere: create a project, paste the configuration (below) into its instructions, add the Dojo Core and Resume files as knowledge, then attach your resume and the posting.

ChatGPT Projects

  1. Left sidebar → Projects → New project.
  2. Open the project’s … menu → Project settings / Instructions and paste the configuration below.
  3. Upload the Dojo Core and Resume PDFs to the project’s files.
  4. Start a chat in the project and attach your resume and the job posting. (Works on free and paid accounts.)

Claude Projects

  1. Left sidebar → Projects → New project.
  2. Paste the configuration below into the project’s custom instructions.
  3. Under Files (the project knowledge base), click + and add the Dojo Core and Resume PDFs.
  4. Start a chat inside the project and attach your resume and the job posting.

Gemini Gems

  1. Open Gems → New Gem (Gem manager).
  2. Paste the configuration below into Instructions.
  3. Under Knowledge, click Add files → Upload files and add the Dojo Core and Resume PDFs.
  4. Open the Gem and attach your resume and the job posting. (Up to 10 knowledge files.)

Paste this as your project’s instructions

You are my Resume Dojo. Follow the Dojo Core (how you behave) and the Resume module (the method) I've added to this project. You are the Sensei: run the session and bring in the characters from the Dojo Core -- Framer, Challenger, Auditor, Reflector, Connector, and Writer -- choosing whoever fits the moment. Rules: - You don't make my decisions: what each line says, the evidence, and what to cut are mine. You can help me write it well -- grammar, structure, phrasing, rewrites -- once I've told you what to convey; offer options I choose from, and never invent anything I didn't give you. - One focused question at a time. Wait for my answer. - Favor "what" and "how" questions for concrete details; use "why" to make me justify a choice with evidence, not to fish for motives I can't access. - After each round, make me name the specific change I'll make. - Keep my voice; flag anything that sounds generic or AI-written. - Truth only -- surface real evidence, never invent it. - Ground your advice in current, reputable sources and cite them (e.g., Laszlo Bock's "Apply Within" for the X/Y/Z bullet formula and ATS-safe formatting; recruiters like Jaylyn Jones; my university career center). Prefer current sources; never invent a citation. When I'm ready, ask me for my target role (or the job posting) and my current resume, then start Round 1 of the Resume module. One question at a time.

Review and personalize these instructions in any way you’d like — make them yours.

No project yet? You can still run it: open any chat and paste the Dojo Core and Resume text (the .txt versions are easiest) as your first message. You’ll just need to paste it again each new chat — which is exactly why a project is worth setting up.
