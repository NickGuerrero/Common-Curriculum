Career Intelligence · Week 2 · Develop & Articulate Your Niche

Resume Story Swap

Learning goals

  • Strengthen your resume with your niche to get a recruiter’s attention.
  • Practice telling your story out loud so you can speak about your experience confidently in an interview.

In pairs, you say the story your resume tells and your partner checks it against your niche. Two ways it breaks:

Drift something on the resume points to a different niche.   Gap a claim about your niche isn’t backed up by anything on the resume.

How it works (~20 min)

  1. Pair up. Open your resume and your Niche Doc.
  2. Speaker (~7 min). From your Niche Doc, say your niche in a few lines — the intersection you bring, your value statement, and the 2–3 role types you’re testing. Then tell the story your resume tells in ~90 seconds, as if you’re explaining it to a recruiter on a video call.
  3. Partner. Name at least one drift and one gap in the resume just presented — point at the exact item, don’t generalize.
  4. Swap, then capture below.
Partner’s lens
  • Does the through-line point at the niche?
  • Which items support it — which are off-target or missing?
  • Where would a recruiter for that niche get confused?
  • Where could this niche describe almost any CS grad? Name one way it isn’t yet specific to this person — and how they’d revise it.

See it in action

The hard part is giving the feedback. Here is a real-shaped resume and the niche it is aiming at, then what a good partner actually says — pointing at the exact item, not a vague “looks good.”

The resume — and the niche it is aiming at
B.S. Computer Science · expected 2026
Experience & Projects
  • Treasurer, Investment Club — ran weekly meetings and tracked the budget.
  • Habit-tracker iOS app (Swift) — a personal project I built to learn mobile.
  • Group web app (React, Node.js) — built it with a team for a class project.
Skills
Python, JavaScript, React, Git

Niche: backend engineer for data-heavy products — APIs, databases, performance.

What a good partner says — point at the item, then ask

Drift “Your top two items are the Investment Club and the Swift app — they point at finance and mobile, so a backend recruiter won’t see your niche. Could those move down, or is there a backend angle to tell instead?”

Gap “Your niche is databases and APIs, but I don’t see SQL, a schema, or an endpoint anywhere — the backend postings I read ask for those first. Which project touched a database, and what did you build?”

Ask “The web-app bullet says you ‘built it with a team’ — what did you own, and did it move a number, like users or response time? That is what a recruiter remembers.”
