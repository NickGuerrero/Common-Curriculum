Career Intelligence · Week 3 · Take-home

Resume Dojo — Review Your Resume

Learning goal

Leave with concrete, evidence-backed resume changes you can defend in your own voice.

This builds on the project you set up in Set Up Your Dojo. If you haven’t set it up yet, do that first.

What to do

  1. Open your Resume Dojo project and attach (or confirm) your current resume and the job posting you’re targeting.
  2. Work through the rounds. The Dojo asks one question at a time — answer with real specifics: what you owned, what changed, the number.
  3. You decide what each bullet should say and the evidence behind it; let the Writer help you word it sharply. Make the edits in your own resume document as you go.
  4. At the end, ask for the recap — your target role, your strongest evidence, your emblem, and what’s still thin.
You direct; let it help with the words. Decide what each line should say and keep the evidence honest — then it’s fine to let the Dojo help with grammar, structure, and phrasing. Just don’t accept a line you couldn’t explain in an interview.

After you finish

Read your revised resume out loud to a peer or TA. The Dojo is your first mirror; a person is your second — they catch what it misses.

Go deeper: Apply Within — Laszlo Bock’s free playbook (former Google SVP of People Operations) the Dojo draws on, including the X/Y/Z bullet formula.
