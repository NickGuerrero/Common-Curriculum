Set Up Your Search Experiment — 10 + 10

Learning goals

  • Turn your job search into a fair experiment you can actually learn from, not a numbers game.
  • Leave with a runnable plan.

This week you test one variable — your bet. Pick the kind that fits you: a geography bet (where you aim) or a sector bet (what industry you aim at). Set up a fair test between your default (A) and your bet (B), send 10 applications into each, and watch what comes back. A “no” or silence is data, not failure.

What to do

  1. Pick your bet, then set your two groups.
    A · Control (your default)

    What you’d do by default — the region, or the kind of company, everyone applies to.

    B · Experimental (your bet)

    Geography: a region you have ties to (you’re local, or it has a sector unique to that region). Sector: an industry where your background is a real differentiator — agtech, climate, health, fintech, gaming…

  2. Write your bet — by hand first, then type it. One clear line:
    Your bet

    Aiming at [B: a region I have ties to, or an industry where I’m a strong fit] beats only chasing [A: my default], because [my real edge in B].

  3. Make it a fair test. Use one resume for both groups, built to make your reason to win B visible (your local tie, or how your background fits that industry). Change only the one thing you’re testing — otherwise a “no” in B won’t tell you anything.
  4. Find your 10 + 10. Line up 10 real postings in group A and 10 in group B, each a genuine fit. Aim for 3–5 quality (not spray-and-pray) per day across the week. A few quick ones on top are fine — just keep them separate. Use your Search Strategy Dojo to help (below).
  5. Name your skill-to-learn. As you read the postings, watch for the one technology or process that keeps showing up. Pick it, and note how you’ll start learning it on your own — that’s how you close the gap your search reveals.
  6. Log everything and start sending. Record each application in your Search Log (a new tab on your application tracker), then start sending. Responses come in over the next two weeks.

Use your Search Strategy Dojo

In your AI Dojo

Add the Search Strategy module to your Dojo (Dojo library), then let it coach you: your bet (geography or sector), your two groups, the fair-test setup, your 10 + 10, and the signal you’ll watch. At the end, paste its summary block straight into your Search Log — no re-typing.

Worked examples — a geography bet and a sector bet

Geography bet — Maria.

Bet: “Aiming at Sacramento, where I’m from and there’s less competition, beats only chasing the Bay Area.” What came back: Bay Area silence; Sacramento, two replies. Lesson + adjustment: the silence wasn’t about her skills, it was the crowded lane — she shifted weight to Sacramento and put “based in Sacramento” at the top of her resume.

Sector bet — Andre.

Bet: “Aiming at agtech, where my data-pipeline work is a real differentiator, beats only chasing general SaaS.” What came back: general SaaS silence; agtech, two replies, one asked about his pipeline. Lesson + adjustment: the sector read his background as directly relevant — he weighted toward agtech and sharpened the bullet that named the fit.

Before you submit, check four things
  • Is B specific? A real region or industry where you have a genuine edge — not “somewhere less crowded.”
  • Could a peer tell A from B? If the two groups blur together, it isn’t a test yet.
  • One resume, edge visible? Same resume to both, with your reason to win B on the page.
  • Is your signal real? Name what you’ll actually watch (reply rate, a recruiter reply, an interview) — and remember silence is data.

Capture & submit

Make a copy of your Search Log (a new Search Strategy tab on your application tracker) and fill in: group (A or B), your bet type (geography or sector), your reason to win B, what you sent and when, the signal you’re watching, and your skill-to-learn.

Submit: the link to your Search Log (set sharing so anyone with the link can view). Then start sending — 3–5 quality applications a day.
