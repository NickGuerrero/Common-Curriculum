# Career Intelligence — Week 2 Plan (v1)

**Theme: Develop & Articulate Your Niche**

Week 1 gave students a first niche. Week 2 makes it real: a resume that says it clearly, and ten actual job postings that prove the niche exists in the market. They leave with a sharper niche, a stronger resume, and their postings sorted into 2–3 role types they can target.

Everything async in Week 2 finishes something they started live. The session is where the thinking happens; the homework is revision, not blank-page work.

---

## Learning outcomes

By the end of Week 2, a student can:

1. **Test their resume against their niche** — find where it points at a different role and where it offers no evidence for the niche yet.
2. **Find 10 niche-aligned job postings and sort them into 2–3 role types** — grounded in language real employers repeat.
3. **Strengthen both the resume and the niche** based on what those two activities surface.

---

## Sync session (~75 min)

**Frame (~10 min).** Recap niche one-liners from Week 1. Set up the day: first you pressure-test the resume you already submitted, then you go find real jobs that prove your niche. Both breakouts feed the work due this week, so do the hard thinking now while a partner is here to help.

### Breakout 1 — Resume Story Swap (~25 min, pairs)

**Why it matters.** A recruiter spends seconds on a resume. If yours doesn't point clearly at your niche, they can't tell what you're for. Saying the story out loud to a peer is the fastest way to hear where it drifts or goes silent.

**How it runs.**
1. Pair up. Each person opens their resume and their niche one-liner.
2. Speaker (~7 min): state your niche, then tell the story your resume tells in about 90 seconds — the through-line, not a full read.
3. Partner names where the resume **drifts** (loud items aimed at a different role) and where it has a **gap** (nothing proves the niche yet).
4. Partner also names where the niche could describe almost any CS grad — i.e., where it isn't yet specific to *this* person — and suggests one way to make it more specific.
5. Speaker writes down 2–3 fixes: for each, either change the resume or sharpen the niche. Swap.

**What good looks like:** the speaker leaves with a short, concrete list — "move the iOS project down, add a backend bullet with a result, tighten niche to mention databases." Specific edits, not general encouragement.

**What to avoid:** reading the whole resume aloud, or a partner who only says "looks good." The partner's job is to find the two or three weakest links.

**Reuse:** `activities/resume-story-swap.html` already runs this, with a worked example (the no-niche resume → drift and gaps appear → one bullet rewritten before/after). One addition needed: the step where the partner flags *where the niche isn't yet unique to the speaker* and suggests a revision.

### Breakout 2 — Job Search & Role Types (~30 min, pairs, screen-share)

**Why it matters.** A niche only counts if real jobs ask for it. Pulling ten postings and seeing the same phrases recur turns "I think I want this" into evidence — and shows whether your niche is one role type or a few.

**How it runs.** One student shares their screen and drives; the partner helps spot matches and push for specifics. Then swap so both get a turn.
1. Driver pastes the job-search prompt below into their AI tool, with their own niche filled in.
2. Together, work the results down to **10 real, currently-open postings** across different companies where the niche is explicit in the responsibilities or requirements. Log them in the tracker.
3. Sort the 10 into **2–3 role types** — group postings that ask for the same kind of work, and name each group in plain words.
4. Swap drivers.

**Job-search prompt (fill in your niche):**

> I'm an early-career candidate targeting roles as a **[YOUR NICHE — e.g., "backend engineer for data-heavy products: APIs, databases, performance"]**. Find 10 real, currently-open job postings from different companies where this niche is explicit in the responsibilities or requirements. For each, give the company, title, link, and the 3–5 exact phrases in the posting that match my niche. Then group the 10 into 2–3 role types and tell me, in one line each, what kind of work distinguishes each type. Don't invent postings — only ones you can link to.

**What good looks like:** ten postings you could actually apply to, each with the matching language pulled out, sorted into a small number of clearly different role types (e.g., "backend/API roles" vs. "data-platform roles").

**What to avoid:** ten links with no notes, postings where the niche match is a guess, or "role types" that are really just a list of company names. If you can't find 10, the niche is probably too narrow — widen it rather than force-fitting jobs.

**Reuse:** `activities/curate-analyze-jobs.html` and its job tracker, plus the sports-tech-engineer worked example. New here: the niche-placeholder prompt above and the explicit "sort into 2–3 role types" step (the current activity logs jobs but doesn't categorize them).

**Wrap (~10 min).** Name this week's async work. Quick round: each student says their single sharpest role type in one sentence.

## Async work (after the session)

### 1. Submit Resume v2 — guided by an AI reflection form

Builds directly on Breakout 1. Use the drifts and gaps your partner found, and turn them into edits.

**The reflection form walks them through:**
1. In one line, what is your niche right now?
2. List each item your partner flagged as drifting (pointing at a different role).
3. List where your resume is silent on your niche — no evidence yet.
4. For each drift: cut it, move it down, or reframe it toward your niche — and write the new line.
5. For each gap: what real experience can you add as evidence? Write the bullet — what you did, the tools, the result.
6. Read the revised resume top to bottom: does the through-line point at your niche in the first third of the page? If not, what moves up?

*AI's role:* push for specifics — if a bullet names no tool or result, ask for one; flag any bullet that still doesn't connect to the niche. Keep the student's own voice; AI confirms, it doesn't rewrite.

**Submit:** the link to the updated resume (the living Google Doc started in Week 1), set so anyone with the link can view.

**Reuse:** `activities/resume-v2.html` (the revision instructions are already written). New: the AI reflection form, in the same pattern as the Week 1 self-check.

### 2. Name your 2–3 role types + update your Niche Template — guided by an AI reflection form

Builds on Breakout 2. Turn the 10 sorted postings into named role types and fold them back into the niche.

**The reflection form walks them through:**
1. Paste your 10 postings (company, title, link).
2. Name your 2–3 role types in plain words.
3. For each role type, what 3–5 phrases or requirements showed up again and again?
4. Which role type has the most evidence in your own background? Which is more aspiration than evidence right now?
5. Rewrite your niche one-liner so it names the role type(s) you're targeting — more specific than last week.
6. What's one thing still missing before this niche is fully backed by evidence?

*AI's role:* if a "role type" is just a list of companies, ask what the work actually is; if two types are nearly identical, push to merge them; if the niche one-liner could describe any CS grad, push for specificity.

**Submit:** the shareable link to the updated Niche Doc / template (Anyone with the link · Commenter).

**Reuse:** `activities/niche-doc-week2.html` (it already asks them to fold in the swap, the postings, and role-type hypotheses, and to tighten the one-liner). New: the AI reflection form for naming role types.

### 3. Week 2 Self-Check (with a handwritten part)

We're dropping the standalone Week 2 handwritten reflection and moving the handwriting into the self-check, so there's one honest look-back instead of two.

**Three questions (keep the existing ones):**
1. **Where are you now?** Name the 2–3 specific role types you're targeting after this week. What in your own background points to them?
2. **What did the market show you?** Across your 10 postings, what skills or words kept coming up — and where does your niche match them or miss them?
3. **What's still rough?** Where does your niche still feel too broad, too narrow, or like a guess — and what's one thing you'll do next to sharpen it?

**Handwritten part:** do question 1 by hand, then photograph it. Writing the role types out by hand forces you to commit to specifics instead of hedging. Type your answers to 2 and 3.

**Submit:** the photo of your handwritten answer to #1, plus typed answers to #2 and #3.

**Reuse:** `activities/week-2-self-check.html` (questions already written). New: the handwritten-step instruction. Retire `activities/handwritten-reflection-week2.html`.
