Start Here: Direct AI Deliberately

Learning goal

Understand Start Here: Direct AI Deliberately and connect it to the work in this module.

Why This Sprint Matters

AI can generate options quickly, but quick output is not the same as trustworthy direction. This sprint teaches you to give AI enough context to be useful, ask for choices rather than one answer, and identify what must be confirmed outside the model.

You will use AI as a thinking partner, but you remain responsible for the goal, standards, choices, and final meaning of the work. Start with your own thinking, use AI to expand and test it, then decide what to keep, reject, revise, or verify.

What You Will Do

  1. Bring forward your Sprint 1 problem frame.
  2. Use Context, Choices, and Confirmation to structure AI conversations.
  3. Critique one AI output and push back on it.
  4. Create an exploration log that shows iterations, assumptions, decisions, and evidence needs.

Two-Week Sprint Map

This sprint spans two course weeks. Use these labels for pacing; Canvas keeps the work in one module.

  • Sprint Week 1: Start here, read the concept page, run the Dojo exploration round, and complete the AI output assessment.
  • Sprint Week 2: Complete the AI peer exchange, submit the AI Exploration Log and 3Cs Application Log, complete the AI self-check, and refine the AI-guided reflection.

Time Guidance

Plan for focused work across two weeks. Short concept reading, Dojo practice, peer exchange, and a final artifact should all serve the same real problem rather than becoming separate assignments.

Working Standard

Specific and honest work is better than polished generic work. Show what you tried, what changed, what evidence you have, and what remains uncertain.

Carry Forward

Keep your prompts, AI outputs, critiques, pushback, revised choices, and confirmation plan. They will support stakeholder validation in Sprint 3.
