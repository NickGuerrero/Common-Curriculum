Concept: Context, Choices, Confirmation

Learning goal

Understand Concept: Context, Choices, Confirmation and connect it to the work in this module.

Context

AI works better when you give it the situation, constraints, audience, purpose, and relevant history. Context is not filler. It is the human knowledge that makes the interaction specific. If the prompt could be used by anyone, it probably needs more context.

Choices

Ask AI to help you surface multiple ways to frame or approach the problem. Good choices include tradeoffs and assumptions. A list of suggestions is not enough. You need to know what each option depends on and what it might cost.

Confirmation

AI can suggest what might be true, but it cannot confirm reality on its own. Confirmation asks what would need to be checked through stakeholders, data, observation, documentation, small tests, or expert review before you trust a direction.

Assessment Is Part Of The Work

Directing AI includes judging the output. You are responsible for identifying what is useful, generic, wrong, risky, or unverified. The strongest AI interaction is often the second or third round, after you push back.

Practice This In Your Work

  • Add three concrete context details before asking for options.
  • Ask for tradeoffs, not only recommendations.
  • Mark assumptions in the output.
  • Name what would have to be confirmed by a real source.
  • Decide what you will carry forward and what you will not trust yet.

Check Your Understanding

Before you move to the next activity, write one sentence in your notes that names the decision you need to make next. If the sentence could apply to anyone, add more of your own context.
