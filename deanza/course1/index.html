Sprint 1: Frame What Is Worth SolvingFraming▸
Module Learning Goals
  • Move from a broad goal or surface request to a problem that is specific enough to test.
  • Use AI to widen and challenge your thinking without handing it final judgment.
  • Create a problem frame grounded in real people, constraints, and evidence.
Start with a real goal from work, job search, community work, caregiving, learning, or another setting where actual people are affected.

Sprint 2: Direct AI DeliberatelyAI Direction▸
Module Learning Goals
  • Give AI enough context, constraints, and standards to make its output useful.
  • Judge AI suggestions instead of accepting the first fluent answer.
  • Document how your choices improved the work.
Builds on the problem frame from Sprint 1. AI can help only after you can explain what needs to be explored.
