What this sprint is about

Direct and delegate — on top of the technical skills you already have.

At its core, Sprint 4 is about becoming better at directing and delegating on top of the technical skills you already have. Three capabilities sit on top — and one foundation makes them all work.

Self-Directed Learner

Learn just-in-time. Pull what you need, when you need it.

Integrative Solver

Cross domains. Connect them to solve.

Adaptive Builder

Build, test, iterate under uncertainty.

Symbiotic Thinking — the foundation. The quality of your conversations (with AI, with peers, with yourself) is what makes the three capabilities above actually work.

Why you’ll defend — and what you’ll defend

The shift you’ll argue, and the moment it happened.

Before this course, you had a picture of what “preparing for AI” meant. Something has shifted since. That shift — and what it cost you to get there — is what you defend.

Written work is where AI is strongest. Live Q&A is where your thinking shows. Format: 5 min presentation + Q&A · W15 or W16.

The belief that changed
One key idea about being prepared for the age of AI you had coming in that has changed. State the before and the after.

45 sec

The moment
The specific point — which sprint, which conversation, which piece of work — where you realized the shift. Not “over time.” A moment.

60 sec

The case
Make the case for your Superagency and Human Value Proposition, with concrete examples from your Sprint 1–4 work. This is the body of the defense.

3 min

Going forward
How you’ll approach work differently because of what changed.

15 sec

Anticipate in Q&A

  • What would a skeptic say about your superagency claim? What’s your strongest counter-evidence?
  • Where in your Sprint 1–4 work did you NOT demonstrate this shift? What does that tell you?
  • If AI capabilities double in the next year, does your case still hold? What would have to change?
  • Which capability — SDL, IS, or AB — is weakest in you right now, honestly?
